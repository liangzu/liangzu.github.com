ICS 45C

How to Learn C++ Better

Learn to search
it is common that you meet a function/keyword/syntax in the program that you are not familiar with. The lecturer taught it but you forgot it. That's where your search engine(e.g., Baidu, Google) comes into play. For example, I don't understand what does the function main even mean, then type "C++ main function" in Google or Baidu will lead me to the anwser. other search examples include: "openlab usage", "how to connect to a server", "linux command xx", "gdb exit command" and so on.
Learn to search more
sometimes Google and Baidu didn't give what you expected, a better place to investigate is the XX offical documentation (XX can be gdb, gcc or so). for example, you can find all functions that C++ provides you in this website. gdb also has its offical website.

ICS 6D

Overview

Discrete Mathematics(ICS 6D) is one of theorical courses in Computer Science, so theorical that you can not understand why the course is as it is until you meet some applications. Fortunately, this course is relatively easy for you because some concepts have been already (probably?) learned by you in high school(e.g., mathematical induction数学归纳法， combinatorics组合计数). Recurrence is difficult to program at the first time but it turns out to be natural and easy once you understand it. I believe that this course will not get you into troubles. In addition, you will find that the homework will be increasingly interesting as the course progress.

Discrete Mathematics and its Applications

boolean logic
heavily used in Digital Circuits(fundamentals of modern computers). as you know, computera are basically comprised of 0-1 series.
Combinatorics
used in Analysis of Algorithms.
Recurrence
Every modern programming language supports Recursion.

related courses

What's taught in ICS 6D is only a small part of Discrete Mathematics, Stanford CS103 is a more complete course that deserves your attention.

Stanford CS103
A substantial course(= ICS6B + ICS6D + more). it's not nessesary to do CS103's homework, but its slides are easy to understand and full of fun. I recommend you to scan the slides to get some insights.
UC Berkely CS70
A combination of Discrete Mathematics and Probability, with a lot of applications(e.g., Stable Marriage), not so useful for your learning ICS6B(but the Probability part is useful for Data Science), just use it as a reference or to open eyes.
