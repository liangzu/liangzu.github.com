This article is just really some links to the classic papers that I've read or I haven't yet. It can serve as a study material for students, and/or a teaching material for instructors.

I marked the degree of the difficulty of these papers by the number of x :

x
means that you can read and understand it if you have some experience of computer science (basic of programming, data structures)
xx
indicates that you can read and understand it if you've taken the corresponding undergraduate-level courses(e.g., Operating System), theory without practice is just enough.
xxx
states that you'd better try some practice before reading it.

Networking

xx The Design Philosophy of the DARPA Internet Protocols
It is sometimes difficult to deduce from these why the protocol is as it is. This paper attempts to capture some of the early reasoning which shaped the Internet protocols.

Operating System

xx The Unix Time-sharing System, 1974
This paper introduces the UNIX and discusses the nature and implementation of the file system and of the user command interface.
xx The Evolution of the Unix Time-sharing System, 1984
This paper presents a technical and social history of the evolution of the Unix.

Compiler

xxx Compiler Construction–The Art of Niklaus Wirth
A collection of tips(not tricks) for building simple, efficient and reliable compilers.
