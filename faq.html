FAQs

Who asked those questions?

This question sounds like "who write this article".Of course those questions is almostly from me, but visitors always have questions as the same as mine. Consequently, those questions are imaginatively asked by you and answered by me.

Why write FAQs instead of self-introduction?

This idea is stolen from Bjarne Stroustrup's Homepage(faqs). I find FAQs make articles easier to organize and lead more interest. I got much fun from bs's faqs, and I hope you can so.

Why do you blog?

"If you can't be googled, you don't exist!". Prof.Matt Might just kidded, "a programmer should blog.".

From my perspective, writing blog can archive 4-R (maybe x-R someday, x > 4):

  • [R]elax myself when suffering from troubles of study and research,
  • w[R]iting is my amateur hobby,
  • articles as a [R]esume when persueing a offer or some others, and most importantly,
  • [R]ecord my life so that I can maintain an invaluable memory.

How often do you blog?

Asserting that blog is just a plus, not a must-have, I will pay main attention on other things (e.g., study, play…), one article per month is an ideal.

How do you blog?

As you can see, I maintain 3 items, which implies the way I organize/presentate my homepage:

Recent plannings
This item lists my plannings or objectives in short term, planning history can also be find here.
Technical articles
This item is plausible, that is, my articles may not be technical enough. I'm not attempt to misguided the visitors, please read my articles critically and rationally.
Life stories
unlike the rational and technical articles, this item aims to be perceptual. Not just listing something ordered by time and/or step by step, it records how I joke, how I survive, how I live, and how I love.

And, as you can't see, I write using Emacs and its Org mode, and publish them through a simple shell script to Github Pages. Thanks them for that they provide such great softwares/services without charging anything. I highly recommend them to you.
